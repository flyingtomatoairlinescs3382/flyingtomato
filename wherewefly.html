Causeway Bay

#Shopping

If shopping were a sport, Causeway Bay would be the home of the Olympic Games. This is where consumerism is out and proud, and every available square inch is devoted to worshipping at the altar of retail. Even though a relatively compact area, it still take at least an entire day to work your way through its endless shopping malls, department stores, boutiques and market stalls.

Ocean Park

#ThemePark#Zoo/Aquarium

Ocean Park Hong Kong is a marine-life theme park featuring animal exhibits, thrill rides and shows. Its impressive ability to offer guests a world-class experience that blends entertainment with education and conserva- tion was confirmed when it became the first Asian winner of the biannual Applause Award, the most prestigious award in the amusement and theme park industry.

The Peak / Victoria Habour

#Landmark

The view from The Peak is one of the most popular attrac- tions in HK. By day your eyes stretch across sparkling skyscrapers and Victoria Harbour all the way to the green hills of New Territories. In early evening this panorama melts into orange before reincarnating as a galaxy of light, shimmering beneath you. And if you listen carefully enough, you can hear Asia’s world city humming below.

Wat Pho

#Landmark

Wat Pho, is located behind the Temple of the Emerald Buddha and a must-do for any first-time visitor in Bang- kok. It's one of the largest temple complexes in the city and famed for its giant reclining Buddha that measures 46 metres long and is covered in gold leaf. It have traditional Thai massage too. Wat Pho is often considered the lead- ing school of massage in Thailand, enjoy!

Sukhumvit

#Shopping

Sukhumvit is the longest road in Thailand. There are many fashionable restaurants, bars, air-conditioned malls, luxury hotels and hip clubs give the area a distinctly modern, cosmopolitan flavour. Acquiring tailor made clothes is one of the joys of visiting Bangkok. This alone could make this city a destination point, as the tailoring here is the most prolific in the world.

Lumpini Park

#Outdoor#Nature

Lumpini Park is an oasis of vegetation in the middle of Bangkok’s urban jungle. One of the oldest recreational parks in Bangkok, it is a popular place for people of all ages and walks to life to convene for exercise, picnics, social gatherings and other pastimes. There are also some animals in the park. You can spot not only birds, street cats and street dogs, but also huge lizards!

Phi Phi Island / Racha Islands

#Outdoor

Phuket is a great place to achieve your PADI certificate. It is rated amongst the top 10 diving destinations in the world and many of the centres in the area boast years of experience. Diving day trips from Phuket to Phi Phi Island and the Racha Islands operate throughout the year. All kind of divers will appreciate the underwater scenery at Phi Phi, the fascinating King Cruiser wreck or Shark Point.

Karon

#Resort

Karon Beach is a very popular resort town located on Phuket island's West coast. Featuring the third longest beach in Phuket, it is a lively destination where vaca- tioners could enjoy a great choice of dining, shopping and nightlife entertainment options. It is family-friendly for it has all the qualities required for anyone to spend a hassle-free holiday under the tropics.

Gibbon Rehabilitation Project

#Nature#Zoo/Aquarium

The GRP was set up in 1992 as a non-government orga- nization (NGO) with the aim of taking captive gibbons and retraining them so that they have a chance of returning to the wild. It is not a petting zoo, but a conservation area to avoid hunters from killing gibbons. Gibbons are an essen- tial part of the health of the jungle. Visitors can understand the habitat and get some conservative msgs here.

Yi Peng

#Landmark

Yi Peng, a festival of lights celebrated mainly in the north of Thailand on 14 Nov this year, which is celebrated during the full moon of November and is Thailand's 2nd most important festival after Songkran. It is a time to be with family and friends and to make merit.People gather near lakes to launch paper lanterns into the sky, and to float their 'krathongs' with loved ones.

Elephant Nature Park

#Nature#Zoo/Aquarium

Elephant Nature Park, a unique conservation project set in Chiang Mai Province, has been in operation since in the 1990's with a goal to provide a sanctuary and elephant rescue center. Their mission is to protect and conserve elephants in a natural environment and to provide a healthy and fruitful life for all those creatures under detail care.

Wat Chedi Luang

#Nature

Wat Chedi Luang (lit. temple of the big stupa) is a Buddhist temple in the historic centre of Chiang Mai. It made up of made up of Wat Chedi Luang, Wat Ho Tham and Wat Sukmin. This was possibly the largest structure in ancient Chiang Mai but the top of the chedi was destroyed by either a 16th-century earthquake or by cannon fire during the recapture of Chiang Mai.

Taipei 101

#Landmark#Shopping

At 509 meters high, this was the world's tallest building from 2004 to 2010. The tower is reminiscent of a pagoda or a stalk of bamboo and designed to withstand typhoons and earthquakes common in Taiwan. Floors one through five are a shopping center and both the indoor (89th floor) and outdoor (91st floor) observation decks offer a 360-de- gree panoramic view.

National Palace Museum

#Heritage#Museum

The National Palace Museum houses the world's largest collection of priceless Chinese art treasures, one which spans China's nearly 5,000-year history. Most of the museum's over 600,000 art objects were part of the Chinese imperial collection, which began over 1,000 years ago in the early Song dynasty. This Museum and the Palace Museum in Beijing share the same roots.

Shilin Night Market

#StreetFood#Shopping

Shilin Night Market is considered to be the largest and most famous night market in the city. It containing mostly food vendors and small restaurants; and the surrounding businesses and shops selling other nonfood items. It is famous for Fried chicken steak, Small Sausage in Large Sausage (大腸包小腸), Oyster omelet, Tempura (甜不辣), Bubble tea (珍珠奶茶) and Oyster vermicelli (蚵仔麵線).

National Museum

#Museum

The National Museum of Natural Science is one of the most popular tourist spots and recreational sites of Taichung, as well as one of the most frequently visited museums in Taiwan. visitors may explore venues such as the Space IMAX Theater, Life Science Hall, Chinese Science Hall, Science Hall, Botanical Garden, and Global Environmental Hall.

Cingjing Farm

#Nature#Zoo/Aquarium

Known as a “Foggy Eden” far away from the city area in the mountains, Qingjing is great for those who enjoy fresh air, lush forests along the plains and hillsides, and beauti- ful starry nights. Qingjing is a favourite spot to interact with farm animals such as cows and sheep. At sunset, the sun interacting with mist and fog over the mountaintops produces amazing weather effects.

Sun Moon Lake

#Outdoor#Nature

The name of the lake is inspired by the beauty of the area. The east side of the lake is round, like the sun, while the west side is long and narrow, like a crescent moon. The aboriginal Thao tribe lives around the lake, and are the proprietors of many local businesses. The town Ita Thao (伊達邵) is located across the lake from the pier area and next to the Sun Moon Lake Ropeway.

The Dome of Light

#Landmark#Shopping

The Formosa Boulevard Station is known for its "Dome of Light", the largest glass work in the world. It is located at the intersection of Jhongjheng Road and Jhongshan Road. This station is currently the largest round-shaped station in the world. The main exits with shell-shape were designed by a Japanese architect, Shin Takamatsu. It is a remembrance of the Formosa Incident.

Pier-2 Art Center

#Museum

It was once an abandoned and forgotten warehouse buried in history due to the move from an industrial based segment to the service sector. However, the Pier-2 area was released and re-born making the region a place where tourist and locals can come together to enjoy fine art. With the collision of an old area and new fine art, Pier-2 becomes an area of new vitality and liveliness.

Liuhe Tourist Night Market

#StreetFood

The Liuhe Night Market is a must visit market if you are in Kaohsiung. It is one of the most popular markets in Taiwan where seafood, handicrafts, clothing, knives, cam- eras and live animals are sold. During the day, Liuhe 2nd Road looks like any other road with thru traffic and store- fronts. But after 6:00pm, the barriers go up and it becomes a pedestrian market.

Tsukiji Fish Market

#Seafood

The Liuhe Night Market is a must visit market if you are in Kaohsiung. It is one of the most popular markets in Taiwan where seafood, handicrafts, clothing, knives, cam- eras and live animals are sold. During the day, Liuhe 2nd Road looks like any other road with thru traffic and store- fronts. But after 6:00pm, the barriers go up and it becomes a pedestrian market.

Akihabara

#Shopping

Akihabara (秋葉原), also called Akiba after a former local shrine, is a district in central Tokyo that is famous for its many electronics shops. In more recent years, Akihabara has gained recognition as the center of Japan's otaku (diehard fan) culture, and many shops and establish- ments devoted to anime and manga are now dispersed among the electronic stores in the district.

Asakusa

#Heritage

Asakusa is the center of Tokyo's shitamachi, one of Tokyo's districts, where an atmosphere of the Tokyo of past decades survives.Asakusa's main attraction is Sensoji, a very popular Buddhist temple. The temple is approached via the Nakamise, a shopping street that has been providing temple visitors with a variety of traditional, local snacks and tourist souvenirs for centuries.

Tokyo Disneyland

#Themepark

Tokyo Disneyland is a theme park based on the films produced by Walt Disney. It was opened in 1983 as the first Disney theme park outside of the United States. Mod- eled after Disneyland in California and the Magic King- dom in Florida, Tokyo Disneyland is made up of seven themed lands and features seasonal decorations and parades.
